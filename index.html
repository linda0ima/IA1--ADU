Online Shopper

with Visual Impairments

Lee, a 28-year-old artist, enjoys online shopping for art supplies and home d√©cor but faces challenges due to his colorblindness and nystagmus. His red-green colorblindness makes it hard to distinguish shades like red, green, and brown, while his nystagmus causes involuntary eye movements, making it difficult to focus on small text or images.

Lee struggles with websites that rely solely on color for information, such as sale prices or product variations. For instance, selecting paint colors or fabric swatches without clear labels can be frustrating. Low contrast, small fonts, and cluttered layouts further complicate his experience by making navigation harder.

He has better experiences on sites with labeled color options, high contrast, and enlarged visuals. Tools like screen magnifiers help him focus on details, while color identification apps assist with distinguishing colors. Text-to-speech tools also reduce eye strain by reading out product descriptions. These adaptations allow Lee to shop confidently for his studio needs.
